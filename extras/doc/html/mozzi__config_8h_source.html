31 /** @ingroup core
32 Holds the audio rate setting.
33 AUDIO_RATE can be \#defined as 16384 or 32768 Hertz in Mozzi/mozzi_config.h.
34 
35 Mozzi's original audio mode, now called STANDARD, uses 16384 Hz, chosen as a
36 compromise between the sample rate (interrupt rate) and sample bitdepth (pwm
37 width), which are interdependent due to the way pulse wave modulation is used to
38 generate the sound output.
39 An AUDIO_RATE of 32768 Hz works in STANDARD_PLUS and HIFI modes.
40 Of course, doubling the sample rate halves the amount of time available to calculate the each sample, so it
41 may only be useful for relatively simple sketches. The increased frequency response can also make
42 unwanted artefacts of low resolution synthesis calculations more apparent, so it's not always a bonus.
43 
44 Another factor which is important for Mozzi's operation is that with AUDIO_RATE
45 being a power of two, some internal calculations can be highly optimised for
46 speed.
47 
48 In STANDARD and STANDARD_PLUS modes, the sample resolution is 488,
49 which provides some headroom above the 8 bit table resolution currently used by
50 the oscillators. You can look at the TimerOne library for more info about how
51 interrupt rate and pwm resolution relate.
52 
53 HIFI audio mode enables much higher quality output by combining signals from pins 9 and 10.
54 For HIFI mode, edit Mozzi/mozzi_config.h to contain \#define AUDIO_MODE HIFI,
55 and comment out \#define AUDIO_MODE STANDARD and \#define AUDIO_MODE STANDARD_PLUS.
56 
57 @todo Possible option for output to R/2R DAC circuit, like
58 http://blog.makezine.com/2008/05/29/makeit-protodac-shield-fo/ .
59 Mozzi-users list has a thread on this.
60 */
61 #define AUDIO_RATE 16384
62 //#define AUDIO_RATE 32768
63 //#define AUDIO_RATE 65536 // try on Teensy3/3.1
